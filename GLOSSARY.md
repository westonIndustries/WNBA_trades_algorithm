# Technical Glossary: The Portability Formula (ETM-527)

[cite_start]This glossary defines the key variables, data sources, and algorithmic components utilized in the Brand Portability Formula model developed for the Winter 2026 term at Portland State University[cite: 27, 49].

---

### Brand Portability Formula Components

**Formula:** χ = Ch ⋅ Ma / (ΔRm + ΔVt)

* **χ (Chi / Brand Portability Score)**: The primary output metric measuring a player's potential impact on team value and revenue when traded to a new market. Higher values indicate greater potential impact.

* **Ch (Career Historical Baseline)**: The player's average annual commercial output from their career data. Acts as the control variable in the formula, representing the player's established brand value.

* **Ma (Market Adjustment Factor)**: A weight based on team value data to measure actual player contribution, adjusted for market tier (1.25 for Tier 1, 1.0 for Tier 2, 0.85 for Tier 3), DMA ranking, and player contribution weight.

* **ΔRm (Revenue Delta)**: The change in player-specific revenue in the new city compared to their career average. Includes merchandise sales changes, TV rating impacts, and ticket premium estimates.

* **ΔVt (Team Value Lift)**: The year-over-year increase in the new team's valuation following the trade, minus the league's average growth rate. Isolates team-specific value increase from league-wide growth.

---

### Market Classification

* **Market Tiers**: Three-tier classification system for WNBA markets:
  - **Tier 1** (Factor: 1.25): Large markets - New York, Los Angeles, San Francisco, Toronto
  - **Tier 2** (Factor: 1.0): Mid-size markets - Seattle, Indiana, Las Vegas, Chicago, Phoenix, Portland
  - **Tier 3** (Factor: 0.85): Smaller markets - Dallas, Washington, Minnesota, Atlanta, Connecticut

* **DMA Ranking**: Designated Market Area rankings used to adjust market factors based on media market size. Lower rankings (e.g., 1 for New York) indicate larger markets.

---

### Estimation Methods

* **Merchandise Sales Estimator**: Calculates player-specific merchandise sales using formula: (Scoring Percentile × Salary Percentile × Market Tier Factor) × League Revenue Baseline × 0.05

* **TV Rating Impact Estimator**: Estimates player-specific TV rating impact using star power score: (Scoring Percentile × 0.4) + (Salary Percentile × 0.3) + (Social Media Index × 0.3), multiplied by league viewership growth and market reach factor.

* **Ticket Premium Estimator**: Estimates player-specific ticket premium using: (Attendance Differential) × (Average Ticket Price) × (Attribution Factor) × (Home Games), where attribution factor combines player performance weight and star power multiplier.

* **Player Revenue Attributor**: Calculates player's attributed share of team revenue change using: (Team Revenue Change) × (Performance Weight) × (Playing Time %), where performance weight = (Player PPG / Team PPG) × 0.6 + (Player Salary / Team Cap) × 0.4

### Core Model Components
* [cite_start]**Portability Index**: A multi-factor model designed to optimize rosters by weighing a player's market power against their financial price[cite: 36, 49].
* [cite_start]**Surplus Value ($V - C$)**: The calculation of a player's hidden ROI, where $V$ represents the value generated by social/brand metrics and $C$ represents the player's contract cost[cite: 35, 46, 47].
* [cite_start]**Structural Break (2026 Shift)**: A recalibration of the model's coefficients to account for the major shift in Revenue Share and the new CBA[cite: 42, 43].
* [cite_start]**The Intercept**: Representing the "culture" or the baseline performance level of the play within the model[cite: 45].
* [cite_start]**The Residual**: The "magic" or intangible factors of the game that numerical data cannot fully convey[cite: 45].

---

### Financial & Contractual Variables
* [cite_start]**`base_salary`**: The primary financial tethering of a player, verified through Her Hoop Stats[cite: 31, 46].
* **Supermax Dummy**: A binary indicator (1 or 0) used to identify if a player is on a Supermax contract and if that contract is undervalued relative to their franchise impact[cite: 32].
* [cite_start]**`rookie_scale`**: The floor of the financial model used to calculate the baseline for entry-level contracts[cite: 38].
* **Revenue Share**: A dynamic float variable updated to reflect the rising tide of league income under the new CBA[cite: 43].

---

### Brand & Social Metrics
* [cite_start]**`follower_count`**: The base social reach extracted via HypeAuditor's API and log-transformed to normalize growth pace[cite: 34, 37].
* **`engagement_rate`**: A metric sourced from STN Digital and Zoomph to measure the depth of a player's social value and audience interaction[cite: 33, 37].
* **`jersey_sales`**: A ranking from Fanatics used to determine "brand liquidity" and direct commercial appeal[cite: 40].
* **`brand_equity`**: A coefficient in the final model used to determine if a team's primary goal is market presence or on-court usage[cite: 44].

---

### Performance & Operational Data
* **`player_id` / `team_iso`**: Primary keys used to initialize the data frame and join roster history across different years[cite: 28, 29].
* [cite_start]**`trade_date`**: Timestamps used to measure the duration of a player's stay with a specific team[cite: 28].
* **Win Shares / PER**: Advanced efficiency metrics pulled from Basketball-Reference to weight on-court performance[cite: 39].
* [cite_start]**`usage`**: On-court usage rates scraped from official WNBA stats[cite: 39].
* [cite_start]**`attendance_delta`**: The change in fan attendance derived from official box scores to see if a player drives ticket sales[cite: 41].

### Testing Methodology

* **Property-Based Testing (PBT)**: Testing approach that validates universal correctness properties across thousands of randomized inputs using the Hypothesis library. The system includes 15 correctness properties that must hold for all valid executions.

* **Unit Testing**: Traditional testing approach that validates specific examples, edge cases, and error conditions using pytest.
