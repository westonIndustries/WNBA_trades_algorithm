To build a professional-grade algorithm, you need data that is verifiable. The WNBA's financial landscape shifted so rapidly between 2024 and 2026 that using "official" sources is the only way to avoid skewed results.Here are the primary sources for the data points I've provided:1. Team Valuations & Revenue (The $V_t$ and $C_h$ inputs)Sportico’s WNBA Valuations: This is the industry gold standard. Their June 24, 2025 report ("Valkyries Make History") is the source for the $500M Golden State valuation and the $269M league average.Forbes Sports Money: Their June 2025 "Skyrocketing Valuations" report provides the 183% average growth figure and the 14.4x revenue multiple used to calculate current value.Front Office Sports (FOS): Excellent for tracking the specific $50M (Golden State/Toronto) and $75M (Portland) expansion fees which set the "market floor" for your 2026 model.2. Market Size & Reach (The $M_a$ inputs)Nielsen DMA Rankings (2024-2025): These rankings (e.g., NY #1, Indy #25) are the official basis for "Market Tiers." You can find the raw household counts on USTVDB.com or Sports Media Watch.Across the Timeline: This is a specialized WNBA database (acrossthetimeline.com) that tracks every attendance figure and broadcast window. Use this for your $\Delta R_m$ (Revenue Delta) to see how many "extra" fans attend when a specific star is in town.
